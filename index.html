A Feasibility Study on Crescent-Shaped Quad Band Microstrip Patch Antenna

A design of a quad-band crescent-shaped microstrip patch antenna is presented. Adaptations in an equivalent circular patch yield new resonant modes nearer to the circular patch, giving a quad-band frequency response and a dual-band response in the Ultra-Wide Band. The proposed design gives 3.1 to 10.6 GHz bandwidth at each frequency with broadside radiation patterns. The crescent shape of the antenna leads to a low profile and compact design, making it suitable for applications where size and weight constraints are essential.

The resonance frequency equation is proposed by defining the resonant altitude at each method in the Crescent shape patch. The frequency was calculated using the exact coordinates close to the simulated result. Due to its unique shape, it is suitable for a wide range of applications where size and weight constraints are essential and where a high level of directivity, wide bandwidth, and circular polarization are required.
